Story: Zeus cheat on wife, zeus turn lover in to bear to hide her from Hera, son of Zeuse's lover becomes hunter, comes across mother as bear and draws bow, Zeus turns son in to bear so he does not kill his mother, alerting Hera, Hera tries to kill bears, Zeus grabs them by their tails, spins them around, then laucnhes them in to sky
You might be more farmiliar with a part of Ursa Major, the big dipper! The big dipper isn't actually a constellation. It's what we call an asterism, a commonly recognized pattern in the sky but not as big as a constellation. In fact, the''big dipper" name has origins in the US. Southern African American slaves drank water from hollowed gords and called the big dipper the "Drinking Gord in the Sky", which always pointed North to freedom. Over time, drinking gord in the sky became big dipper, and today we call it the big dipper
